Divergence Meter Build

On this page, I will be updating everything I do to build the Divergence Meter. (All credits go to TomTitor, who made all the instructions, codes, designs, circuits… everything you will see here, components, circuits, everything is from his page, so I thank him for all of that.)

30/07/25

I’ve created a list based on the original one with all the necessary electronic components. One of them, the 1364 - HVPS Horizontal, needs to be ordered from the US (the shipping cost is $22, rip. But I guess it’s worth it).

As for the PCBs, the price can vary a lot depending on the supplier, shipping, and your location. I’m currently considering ordering them from JLCPCB, which seems like a very good option. They require a minimum of 5 boards per design (there are two designs for this project), but the upside is that the total cost, including taxes and shipping, is around €50—roughly half of what it would cost to order from the project's original site. They actually recommend using local PCB manufacturers depending on where you live.

Once I completed the list, I ordered the components with the financial help of a friend who is also eager to see this project become a reality, for which I'm very grateful. Most of the parts were ordered through Mouser.es, a site recommended by the original project creators. In my case, they offer free shipping from Europe, and the total cost was between €70 and €80.
